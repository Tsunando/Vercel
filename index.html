Regulating Tobacco Products in Canada

Here in Canada, the Tobacco and Vaping Products Act or TVPA helps regulate the manufacture, sale, labelling, and promotion of tobacco products as well as vaping products that are being sold in the country. Its regulations aim to advance the strategy of the government to protect its citizens from tobacco-related death and disease. Some key purposes of TVPA include:
  • Protecting the health of Canadians in light of the mounting evidence implicating tobacco use as the key reason behind debilitating and fatal diseases
  • Protecting young persons and others from inducements to use tobacco products and the risks associated with them
  • Protecting the health of young persons through restricting access to tobacco products
  • Preventing the public from being deceived or misled with respect to the health hazards of using tobacco products
  • Boosting public awareness of the health hazards of using tobacco products
Other laws that regulate tobacco products include the following:
  1. Non-smokers’ Health Act: This act addresses issues related to using tobacco, vaping and cannabis products, including secondhand smoke and aerosol, in federally-regulated workplaces like banks, commercial aircraft, and federal government offices. 
  2. Excise Act: Tobacco products manufactured in Canada or imported into Canada must be subject to excise duties or taxes. 
  3. Provincial, territorial, and municipal laws: Provincial, territorial, and municipal governments must regulate tobacco and vaping products and their use. They have the power to prohibit tobacco use in certain public spaces, add more taxes, and so on.

Key Factors Affecting the Prices of Tobacco

There are numerous factors that could affect the general prices of tobacco products in Canada. Some of these factors include the following:

Federal and Provincial Taxes

One of the primary factors that affect the prices of tobacco products in Canada is tobacco taxation. The federal government, as mentioned, aims to protect Canadians from the harmful health risks caused by tobacco products. Therefore, they impose excise duties on cigarettes, cigars, and smokeless tobacco, pushing their prices up. Provinces then add their own taxes, which is the reason why the same products may cost differently across regions. 

Strict Government Restrictions

The prices of tobacco products may also be impacted by strict regulations by the government, particularly on their sales, packaging, and marketing. As Canada enforces standardized plain packaging, health warning labels, and restrictions on flavoured tobacco, manufacturers have to adhere to the imposed regulations and restrictions, leading to an increase in production costs. 

Rising Costs of Raw Tobacco

Cigarettes, cigars, and smokeless tobacco can only maintain their quality if they are made from high-quality raw ingredients. The cost of raw tobacco, however, has continued to increase over the past few years due to global supply chain disruptions, tariffs, and transportation costs. You can then expect specialty or premium tobacco products to become more expensive since they rely heavily on high-quality leaves and top-notch processing.

The Existence of the Black Market

Illegal tobacco sales create an underground market, or black market, that competes directly with legal retailers and shops. Some consumers turn to these lower-cost alternatives, which reduces demand for taxed products. This then influences and affects the pricing strategies of legal retailers and shops. Fortunately, the government is now keen on curbing illegal tobacco sales, protecting tax revenue and allowing legitimate retailers to earn.
